What is CRESTA?

CRESTA is an innovative cooling architecture that combines indirect evaporative cooling, closed-loop recirculation, and waste heat recovery to achieve significantly lower PUE and reduced water consumption compared to conventional CRAC-based systems.

The system uses a two-stage approach: primary air-to-liquid heat exchange at the rack level, followed by evaporative cooling of the secondary loop—enabling free cooling in most climates.

CRESTA Advantages

  • Indirect evaporative cooling — no moisture in data hall
  • Rack-level heat capture — higher ΔT, smaller ducts
  • Closed-loop secondary — minimal makeup water
  • Waste heat recovery option — district heating / DHW
  • ASHRAE A1–A4 compatible — flexible setpoints

Conventional CRAC Limitations

  • Room-level cooling — low ΔT, high airflow
  • Direct expansion or chilled water — high energy
  • Humidification/dehumidification — water intensive
  • Heat rejected to ambient — no recovery
  • Fixed setpoints — less flexibility
